# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective clients, startup founders, NGOs, local businesses, and collaborators evaluating Ntsinzi Francois as a full-stack developer who can turn unclear digital ideas into shipped web, mobile, dashboard, and business-system products. They arrive with business pressure, trust questions, scope uncertainty, and a need to see proof that the work can be both technically solid and visually premium.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to convert trust into contact. It should show that Ntsinzi can plan, design, build, deploy, and support digital products across frontend, backend, mobile, DevOps, and client delivery. Success looks like visitors quickly understanding the range of work, seeing real project evidence, feeling confidence in the delivery process, and choosing a build path or contact action.

## Brand Personality

Technical premium, confident, product-minded. The voice should feel sharp and capable without becoming cold: cinematic code energy, strong execution, practical delivery language, and enough warmth to reassure clients who may not know how to scope software yet.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic developer portfolio templates, vague "modern SaaS" pages, soft glassmorphism, purple-blue AI gradients, decorative code noise with no business meaning, and portfolio pages that feel like a resume pasted into cards. Avoid making the site feel like a course platform unless that metaphor is serving the portfolio narrative directly.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with proof, not claims: shipped projects, screenshots, outcomes, pricing clarity, and concrete capabilities should do the persuasive work.
2. Make technical craft feel valuable: code, systems, motion, and tooling should read as business confidence rather than decoration.
3. Preserve a high-contrast identity: the portfolio should feel energetic, precise, and memorable while keeping text readable and actions obvious.
4. Keep the path to hiring clear: navigation, project filtering, pricing, and contact should reduce uncertainty for serious prospects.
5. Balance ambition with trust: motion and visual intensity should suggest premium craft without obscuring content, performance, or accessibility.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Preserve strong contrast, keyboard-accessible navigation and controls, visible focus states, readable responsive layouts, semantic interactive elements, and reduced-motion alternatives for any substantial animation or scroll-driven effect.
